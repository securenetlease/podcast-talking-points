Closing Segment — Key Talking Points
💰
Cap-Rate Expectations
  • Modest 5–15 bps compression expected across most sectors — but the 10-year near 4.25% is a hard ceiling
  • Net lease already sub-8% for quality assets — institutional competition is closing that window fast
  • Office is the exception — pricing near land value creates asymmetric upside for buyers with a conversion thesis
🥊
Deal Competition
  • Deal volume projected up 16% YoY — multifamily, net lease, and grocery-anchored retail are the most competitive right now
  • 74% of institutional investors want to buy more this year but very few want to sell — quality inventory is squeezed
  • Distressed office is the most buyer-friendly environment in the market — low competition, motivated sellers, values near the floor
🏦
Debt Availability
  • Agency debt is the best story: Fannie and Freddie both raised multifamily caps to $88B — a 20% increase
  • Life company debt wins for core deals: ~170 bps spreads, 50–65% LTV, most competitive execution available
  • CMBS workable at ~250 bps but rising delinquencies are making lenders more selective — bridge lending on office or lodging is extremely difficult
  • Private credit is filling the gap left by regional banks and is very active where traditional lenders won't go
🗺️
Relative Value: Best Sectors & Markets
  • ✅ Best Now Necessity net lease, open-air grocery retail, cold-chain industrial — Dallas, Nashville, Charlotte, Tampa
  • ✅ Selective Sun Belt multifamily at peak supply; senior housing acquisitions
  • ⚠️ Caution Coastal industrial in SoCal and Pacific Northwest — tariff headwinds are real
  • 🔑 Contrarian Distressed office in Chicago, Denver, D.C. at 10–20 cents on the dollar for conversion buyers
  • 🚫 Avoid Class B/C office with no conversion path; lodging with near-term CMBS maturities
